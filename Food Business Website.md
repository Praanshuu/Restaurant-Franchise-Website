# Structure

# **Website Structure — 11 Sections**

*Ordered for maximum emotional and conversion flow.*  
Click any section to expand its rationale and content guide.

---

## **01\. Hero — The Mood Opener**

**Full-screen atmosphere. Not “welcome,” but a feeling.**

* **Headline:** Occasion-based, not product-based  
  *Example:* “For nights that deserve more than delivery.”  
* **Visual:** Cinematic full-bleed photo or video — real food, real people, real light  
* No generic taglines. No “best food in town.”  
  → One sentence that creates a craving  
* **Primary CTA:** “See the menu” or “Reserve a table” — single clear action  
* **Secondary (ambient):** Hours, location (minimal, below the fold)

**Focus:** Emotion

---

## **02\. The “When Would You Come Here” Strip**

**Real occasions, not generic categories.**

* 3–5 scrollable occasion cards:  
  *Date night, Birthday brunch, Lazy Sunday family lunch, After-work unwinding*  
* Each card \= visual \+ 1-line description  
  → No bullet lists. Just a vibe  
* Answers: *“When would I choose this place?”*  
* Replaces: “Lunch / Dinner / Takeaway”  
  → Those are logistics, not decisions

**Focus:** Intent

---

## **03\. Signature Section — “Why This Place”**

**The one thing that makes this place different.**

* Not a feature list — a story or statement  
  *Examples:*  
  “We roast our own beans every morning”  
  “Our dough takes 3 days”  
* Short video or photo-with-text layout  
  → Behind-the-scenes, not marketing shots  
* Answers: *“What makes this place memorable?”*  
* Founder note or head chef voice  
  → Human, first-person, not corporate

**Focus:** Trust

---

## **04\. Menu Preview — Curated, Not Complete**

**Showcase 4–6 signature items that define the brand.**

* Not the full menu — create curiosity and expectation  
* Each item:  
  → Real photo \+ dish name \+ 1-line description (flavour, not ingredients)  
* Example:  
  “Truffle arancini that disappear before the main course”  
  *(Not: “Arancini: rice, truffle, parmesan”)*  
* **CTA:** “View full menu” → PDF or dedicated page  
* Optional: Price range indicator (not exact pricing)

**Focus:** Craving

---

## **05\. Social Proof — Real Moments, Not Ratings**

**Show people, not stars.**

* Instagram-style mosaic of real customer photos (with permission)  
* 1–2 handpicked Google/Zomato reviews  
  → Not average rating, but meaningful quotes  
* Optional: Google rating badge (only if high — 4.6+)  
* Avoid: “50,000 happy customers” — irrelevant locally  
* Answers: *“Are real people enjoying this?”*

**Focus:** Social Proof

---

## **06\. Experience Section — “What It Feels Like Inside”**

**Show ambience without saying “ambience.”**

* 3–4 high-quality interior/atmosphere photos  
  → Horizontal scroll or grid  
* Sensory copy: what guests hear, see, smell  
  *Example:*  
  “Warm lighting, unhurried pace, music that never competes with conversation”  
* Avoid: “Great ambience and cozy interiors”  
* Optional:  
  → 360° Google Maps embed  
  → Short ambience video reel

**Focus:** Atmosphere

---

## **07\. Order / Reserve — The Primary Conversion Block**

**Two clear paths: dine-in or order.**

* Reservation form (or Dineout/Easytable embed)  
  → Minimal fields: name, date, time, party size  
* Direct order CTA  
  → Prefer own system (not just Zomato/Swiggy — or include both)  
* Highlight benefits of direct ordering:  
  → “No platform fees” / “Faster dispatch”  
* WhatsApp order button (very effective in Indian markets)  
* Must be: fast, error-free, mobile-first

**Focus:** Conversion

---

## **08\. Offers / Loyalty — Reason to Come Back**

**Retention over gimmicks.**

* Include only real promotions (no fake urgency)  
* Examples:  
  → Happy hour  
  → Weekend specials  
  → Loyalty cards  
  → Birthday offers  
* Newsletter / WhatsApp signup with honest promise:  
  → “Get early access to our seasonal menu”  
* Builds a direct customer list (high-value asset)

**Focus:** Retention

---

## **09\. Catering / Events — B2B Revenue Stream**

**Often ignored, high-value section.**

* Corporate orders, private events, bulk catering  
* Problem-first framing:  
  → “Hosting an office lunch?”  
  → “Celebrating a milestone?”  
* Simple inquiry form or WhatsApp link  
* Include only if actually offered

**Focus:** B2B

---

## **10\. Find Us — Location Done Right**

**Context, not just a map.**

* Google Maps embed \+ nearby landmarks  
  *Example:* “2 minutes from City Mall, look for the orange awning”  
* Practical info for Indian users:  
  → Parking availability  
  → Auto-rickshaw access  
  → Public transport  
* Honest hours:  
  → “Closed early on Mondays”  
  → “Last order at 9:30 PM”  
* One-tap call button (mobile-friendly)  
* “Open in Maps” link for navigation

**Focus:** Access

---

## **11\. Footer — Navigation \+ Trust Signals**

**The safety net for bottom-scroll users.**

* Social links (Instagram primary for food brands)  
* FSSAI license number (often ignored, builds trust)  
* Quick links: Menu, Reserve, Contact, Catering  
* One-line brand tagline  
* Keep it clean — avoid excessive legal clutter

**Focus:** Foundation

---

# Theme

# **Design Theme — *Warm Editorial***

**Philosophy:**  
Premium without intimidation.  
Earthy without being rustic.  
Clean without being cold.

This theme works across **restaurants, cafés, and bakeries**, and adapts per brand using an **accent color swap system**.

---

## **Color System**

| Role | Name | Hex | Usage |
| ----- | ----- | ----- | ----- |
| Primary Text | **Ink** | `#1C1814` | Headlines, body text |
| Background | **Parchment** | `#F7F2EC` | Main canvas |
| Primary Accent | **Saffron** | `#C4763A` | CTAs, highlights |
| Secondary Accent | **Herb** | `#3D6B5A` | Tags, badges |
| Surface | **Cream** | `#EDE8E1` | Cards, muted sections |
| Secondary Text | **Stone** | `#7A7069` | Metadata, captions |
| Dark Base | **Espresso** | `#2A2420` | Footer, dark sections |
| Contrast | **White** | `#FFFFFF` | Overlays, highlight cards |

### **Accent Color Swap System**

* Replace **Saffron (`#C4763A`)** with a brand-specific color:  
  * Deep burgundy → wine bar  
  * Dusty rose → patisserie  
  * Terracotta → regional cuisine

→ The entire palette adapts while maintaining structure and harmony.

---

## **Typography Pairing**

### **1\. Playfair Display *(Serif)***

* **Use for:**  
  Headlines, section titles, dish names, brand moments  
* **Feel:** Romantic, editorial, premium  
* **Weights:** 400, 600

---

### **2\. DM Sans *(Sans-serif)***

* **Use for:**  
  Body copy, navigation, CTAs, captions  
* **Feel:** Friendly, modern, highly legible  
* **Weights:** 300, 400, 500

---

### **3\. Cormorant Garamond Italic *(Optional)***

* **Use sparingly for:**  
  Pull quotes, founder notes, occasion labels  
* **Purpose:** Adds intimacy and personality  
* **Style:** Italic only

---

## **Typography Scale**

* **H1 / Hero**  
  *Evenings made for staying*  
* **H2 / Section**  
  *Our signature dishes*  
* **H3 / Item**  
  *Truffle arancini*  
* **Body**  
  *Made fresh every morning from stone-ground flour.*  
* **Caption**  
  *Open Tuesday–Sunday · 12pm–10:30pm*

---

## **Layout Principles**

### **1\. Generous Whitespace**

* 80–120px spacing between sections  
* Let content breathe  
* Food photography needs visual room  
* A cramped layout reduces perceived quality

---

### **2\. Full-Bleed Imagery Leads**

* Photography does the heavy lifting  
* Alternate sections:  
  * Photo-heavy  
  * Text-heavy  
  * Photo-heavy  
* Avoid stacking multiple text-heavy sections

---

### **3\. Dark Footer, Light Body**

* Use **Espresso (`#2A2420`)** for footer  
* Keep main body light (**Parchment**)  
* Creates a strong visual endpoint  
* Adds warmth and contrast

---

### **4\. Visual Rhythm**

* Mix:  
  * Large imagery  
  * Short editorial text  
  * Breathing space  
* Avoid dense blocks of information

---

### **5\. Editorial, Not Template-Like**

* Think: magazine layout, not app UI  
* Prioritize storytelling over structure  
* Each section should feel intentional, not repeated

---

# Conversion

# **Conversion System — 9 Mechanisms**

*Every section earns its place by answering a real customer question.*  
Each mechanism below explains **what it does, why it works, and where it fits.**

---

## **01\. First-Second Hook**

**Win attention in under 3 seconds.**

* Hero headline must convey a *feeling* in **under 8 words**  
* Visitors decide to scroll or leave almost instantly  
* Avoid generic welcomes

**Example:**  
✔ “For the meal that deserves a full evening”  
✘ “Welcome to Spice Garden”

**Purpose:** Drive immediate emotional engagement

---

## **02\. Occasion-First Navigation**

**Design around intent, not structure.**

* Replace category-based menus with **occasion triggers**  
* Speak to *why* the user is visiting

**Example:**  
✔ “Planning a birthday?”  
✘ “Events”

* Aligns with real customer decision-making

**Purpose:** Capture high-intent users faster

---

## **03\. WhatsApp as Primary Mobile CTA**

**Meet users where they already act.**

* WhatsApp buttons outperform forms in the Indian market  
  → **3–5× higher conversion rates**  
* Use a **pre-filled message** to reduce friction

**Example:**  
“Hi, I’d like to reserve a table for…”

* Ideal for:  
  * Reservations  
  * Quick inquiries  
  * Orders

**Purpose:** Maximize mobile conversions

---

## **04\. Menu as Appetite Trigger (Not Information Sheet)**

**Sell the craving, not the catalog.**

* Show a **curated preview (max 6 items)**  
* Full menu remains one click away  
* Focus on desire, not completeness  
* Visitors who *feel hungry* are significantly more likely to act immediately  
  → (\~40% higher action rate)

**Purpose:** Trigger emotional decision-making

---

## **05\. Authentic Social Proof Over Ratings**

**Show experience, not numbers.**

* One real customer moment \> star ratings  
* Use:  
  * Genuine photos  
  * Select meaningful reviews  
* Avoid over-reliance on:  
  * Ratings badges  
  * Generic testimonials

**Purpose:** Build trust through relatability

---

## **06\. Direct Order Benefit Framing**

**Give users a reason to choose your channel.**

* Clearly communicate advantages of ordering directly

**Examples:**

* “No platform charges”  
* “Faster delivery”  
* “Better portion value”  
* Reduces dependency on platforms like Zomato/Swiggy

**Purpose:** Shift user behavior to owned channels

---

## **07\. Location with Wayfinding Language**

**Reduce friction for first-time visits.**

* Don’t rely on maps alone  
* Add **familiar landmarks and context**

**Example:**  
✔ “2 minutes from Phoenix Mall, opposite HDFC Bank”  
✘ Just a map pin

* Especially important in Indian urban environments

**Purpose:** Increase physical visit conversions

---

## **08\. Catering as B2B Conversion Layer**

**Unlock high-value, low-competition revenue.**

* Target:  
  * Corporate lunches  
  * Office events  
  * Weddings & bulk orders  
* Typically:  
  * Higher ticket size  
  * Lower acquisition cost  
* Use:  
  * Simple inquiry form  
  * WhatsApp contact

**Purpose:** Capture overlooked revenue streams

---

## **09\. WhatsApp / Newsletter List Building**

**Build a long-term growth asset.**

* Direct audience \= zero-cost future marketing  
* Avoid generic “Subscribe” messaging

**Example:**  
✔ “Get our seasonal menu first”  
✘ “Join our newsletter”

* Channels:  
  * WhatsApp opt-in  
  * Email list

**Purpose:** Retention and repeat business

---

## **System Summary**

* **Top of funnel:** Hook \+ Occasion framing  
* **Mid funnel:** Craving \+ Trust (menu \+ social proof)  
* **Bottom funnel:** Conversion (WhatsApp, direct order, location)  
* **Long-term:** Retention (loyalty \+ list building)

# Copy Rules

# **Copywriting Rules**

*Every word on this website is a sales decision.*  
These rules ensure your copy stays **distinct, memorable, and conversion-focused**.

---

## **01\. Occasion-First, Category-Second**

**Lead with *when*, not *what*.**

* Start with the moment or situation the customer relates to  
* Avoid leading with operational descriptions

**Example:**  
✔ “For the Sunday that belongs to no one’s schedule”  
✘ “Casual dining restaurant open 7 days”

**Principle:** People choose occasions before categories

---

## **02\. Sensory Language Over Adjective Stacking**

**Make them feel it, not evaluate it.**

* Describe:  
  * Taste  
  * Texture  
  * Sound  
  * Atmosphere  
* Avoid empty descriptive adjectives

**Example:**  
✔ “The kind of bread that demands you eat it before it cools”  
✘ “Fresh artisan bread made with premium ingredients”

**Principle:** Sensation creates craving

---

## **03\. No Fake Superlatives**

**Replace claims with stories.**

* Avoid words that require proof but provide none:  
  * “Best”  
  * “World-class”  
  * “Award-winning”

**Example:**  
✔ “Our pizza is the reason Tuesday nights have regulars”  
✘ “Best pizza in Raipur”

**Principle:** Specificity builds trust

---

## **04\. Every CTA Is a Next Step, Not a Command**

**Frame actions around the customer’s outcome.**

* Focus on what the user gets, not what they must do

**Example:**  
✔ “Secure your table”  
✔ “Start your order”  
✘ “Book now”  
✘ “Click here”  
✘ “Submit”

**Principle:** Good CTAs feel like progress, not effort

---

## **05\. The Founder / Chef Voice Section**

**Sound like a human, not a template.**

* One section should feel:  
  * Personal  
  * Specific  
  * Slightly imperfect

**Example:**  
✔ “We started because we couldn’t find the kind of biryani we grew up eating”  
✘ “We are passionate about quality food”

✘ “Our team is passionate about serving you”

**Principle:** Authenticity outperforms polish

---

## **06\. One Clear Differentiator (Repeated With Intent)**

**Say one thing that only you can say.**

* Identify what makes this place different from every nearby competitor  
* Reinforce it across key sections:  
  * Hero  
  * Signature section  
  * Footer tagline

**Example Approach:**  
✔ One clear, specific idea repeated in context  
✘ “We believe in quality, hygiene, service, and taste”  
→ (When everything is important, nothing stands out)

**Principle:** Clarity beats breadth

---

## **System Summary**

* **Hook with emotion** → Occasion-first framing  
* **Build desire** → Sensory language  
* **Earn trust** → Specificity over claims  
* **Drive action** → Outcome-based CTAs  
* **Humanize brand** → Founder voice  
* **Stay memorable** → One differentiator

# Technical Blueprint

**Technical Blueprint**

**Stack:** HTML \+ CSS \+ JavaScript (Vanilla)  
**Philosophy:** Fast, lightweight, no dependencies  
**Target:** Loads in under 2 seconds on 4G and runs smoothly on a ₹8,000 Android device

---

## **Performance Rules**

### **01\. Images — Optimized for Speed**

* Use **WebP format** for all images  
* Enable `loading="lazy"` for all non-critical images  
* **Hero image:**  
  * Preloaded (critical asset)  
* Size constraints:  
  * Max **150KB per image on mobile**  
* Use `srcset` for responsive image delivery

**Goal:** Fast load without compromising visual quality

---

### **02\. Fonts — Minimal & Efficient**

* Use only **2 Google Fonts families**  
* Maximum **3 font weights total**  
* Add preconnect:

\<link rel="preconnect" href="https://fonts.googleapis.com"\>

* Prevent FOUT (Flash of Unstyled Text)

**Goal:** Maintain design quality without performance cost

---

### **03\. JavaScript — Lightweight Only**

* No jQuery or heavy libraries  
* Use **vanilla JavaScript** for:  
  * Smooth scrolling  
  * IntersectionObserver animations  
  * Basic form validation  
* Total JS bundle:  
  * **Under 20KB (uncompressed)**

**Goal:** Keep interactions smooth and fast

---

## **Interaction System**

### **01\. Sticky Navigation (Smart Scroll Behavior)**

* Navbar hides on scroll down  
* Reappears on scroll up  
* Highlights active section

**Benefit:** Maximizes screen space while maintaining navigation access

---

### **02\. Scroll-Triggered Fade-In**

* Use **IntersectionObserver**  
* Animation:  
  * 300ms fade-in  
  * 20px upward translate  
* Keep motion subtle (no bounce or excessive effects)

**Principle:** Subtle \= premium

---

### **03\. Persistent WhatsApp Floating Button**

* Fixed position: **bottom-right**  
* Visible across all pages  
* Includes **pre-filled message**  
* Styled using accent color

**Note:**  
This is often the **highest-converting element** on Indian food websites

---

### **04\. Horizontal Scroll (Occasion Cards)**

* Use **CSS scroll-snap** (no JS required)  
* Applies to Section 02 (Occasion strip)  
* Mobile-first interaction:  
  * Swipeable cards  
  * App-like feel

**Benefit:** Native, smooth UX without performance cost

---

## **Adaptability System**

### **Brand Variables File (Core System)**

All design tokens live in:  
/css/variables.css

*   
* Includes:  
  * Colors  
  * Typography  
  * Spacing  
  * Radius / shadows

### **Instant Theme Switching**

* Change one variable:

\--color-accent: \#C4763A; /\* Saffron \*/

* Swap to:

\--color-accent: \#6B1F2A; /\* Burgundy \*/

→ Entire website updates instantly

**Time to re-theme:** Under 5 minutes
